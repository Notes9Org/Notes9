Catalyst answer across these 12 papers

Growth-phase regulation has a substantial effect on carotenoid titer in S. cerevisiae, with most studies showing that shifting biosynthesis to the stationary phase through promoter swaps or two-stage fermentations increases β-carotene yield by 2–8×. The strongest gains come from coupling stationary-phase promoters (e.g., HXT1, ADH2, GAL1 with carbon-source switching) to the crtYB/crtE/crtI cassette12, while constitutive expression during exponential growth tends to redirect acetyl-CoA flux away from ergosterol intermediates3.

Three of these studies also report that nitrogen limitation applied during late exponential phase enhances accumulation, likely by extending the production window24. Effects are host-strain dependent — BY4742 derivatives respond more strongly than CEN.PK2-1C under matched conditions3.

Engineering a growth-phase-dependent biosynthetic pathway for carotenoid production in Saccharomyces cerevisiae

Su B, Song D, Yang F, Zhu H · J. Ind. Microbiol. Biotechnol. (2020)

Results

To investigate whether decoupling carotenoid biosynthesis from primary growth would improve titer, we constructed two strain panels differing only in the promoters driving crtYB and crtE. The constitutive panel used TDH3 promoters and the growth-phase panel used the late-log–induced HXT1 promoter.

After 48 h of aerobic shake-flask fermentation in YPD medium, strains expressing crtYB and crtE under the late-log HXT1 promoter produced 6.5-fold higher β-carotene titer (4.8 mg/g DCW) than the constitutive TDH3 controls (0.74 mg/g DCW) when sampled at 48 h. The growth-phase strains reached their carotenoid peak in late-exponential to early-stationary phase (24–32 h), whereas the constitutive strains plateaued earlier and showed no further accumulation after the diauxic shift.

The fold-improvement was reproducible across three independent biological replicates and was robust to two different background hosts (BY4742 and CEN.PK2-1C), though absolute titers differed between hosts (Table 1).

Two-stage fermentation

We next asked whether further productivity gains were available from manipulating culture conditions. Two-stage fermentation, in which cells are grown to mid-log in glucose-rich medium and then shifted to nitrogen-limited medium with a non-fermentable carbon source, has been shown to extend the production window in other secondary-metabolite systems.

— 7 —

Figure 4: Two-stage fermentation results

Two-stage fermentation with nitrogen limitation applied at 36 h further increased accumulation to 14.2 ± 1.1 mg/g DCW, suggesting that extending the production window is more effective than raising peak flux. The improvement was consistent across both BY4742 (14.2 mg/g) and CEN.PK2-1C (11.7 mg/g) backgrounds, though BY4742 retained an absolute advantage.

We did not observe a significant additional benefit from coupling HXT1 to crtI; the rate-limiting step in our hands remained the crtE-encoded geranylgeranyl pyrophosphate synthase. This suggests that future engineering should prioritize upstream MEP/MVA flux rather than further promoter swaps on the downstream carotenoid cassette.

You
How does this paper handle growth-phase regulation, and what titer did they hit?
✦ Catalyst
They use the late-log-induced HXT1 promoter on crtYB and crtE (with GAL1 on crtI), which shifts biosynthesis into late exponential and early stationary phase. This produced a 6.5× titer improvement over the constitutive TDH3 baseline — 4.8 mg/g DCW vs 0.74 mg/g DCW at 48 h [¶3, p.4].

Adding two-stage fermentation with nitrogen limitation at 36 h pushed the titer further to 14.2 ± 1.1 mg/g DCW [Fig. 4, p.7].

Worth noting: they tried coupling HXT1 to crtI as well but saw no additional benefit — they argue the rate-limiting step is GGPP synthase (crtE), not the downstream cassette [Discussion, p.9].

Does the same effect hold for CEN.PK2-1C, which we're using?
✦ Catalyst
Yes — both effects (growth-phase regulation and two-stage fermentation) reproduce in CEN.PK2-1C, but with lower absolute titers than BY4742. From the paper:
  • Growth-phase fold-improvement: reproducible across both hosts.
  • Two-stage absolute titers: BY4742 = 14.2 mg/g · CEN.PK2-1C = 11.7 mg/g [Fig. 4, p.7]
This matches what we saw in Lab Note BC-TEST-001 Final Results from our own Round 1 — BY4742 outperformed CEN.PK2-1C by ~30%.
