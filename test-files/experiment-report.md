# Experiment Report - Protein Crystallization Study

## Objective
Determine optimal crystallization conditions for Target Protein X using hanging drop vapor diffusion method.

## Materials and Methods

### Reagents
- Protein sample: 10 mg/mL in Tris buffer (pH 7.4)
- Precipitant: PEG 3350 (20-30% w/v)
- Buffer: 0.1 M Tris-HCl pH 7.0-8.5
- Additive: 0.2 M NaCl

### Procedure
1. Prepare protein drops (2 μL protein + 2 μL reservoir solution)
2. Incubate at 20°C
3. Monitor crystal growth daily for 14 days
4. Image crystals using polarized light microscopy

## Results

### Crystal Growth Observations

**Day 3:**
- Wells C4, C5, D3 showed microcrystals
- Crystal size: 20-50 μm
- Temperature maintained at 20±1°C

**Day 7:**
- Crystals grew to 100-150 μm
- Well-defined hexagonal morphology
- No contamination detected

**Day 14:**
- Final crystal size: 200-300 μm
- Suitable for X-ray diffraction
- Selected 5 crystals for data collection

### Optimal Conditions
- PEG 3350: 25% (w/v)
- pH: 7.8
- NaCl: 0.2 M
- Temperature: 20°C

## Conclusions

Successfully obtained diffraction-quality crystals. Optimal conditions identified for reproducible crystallization. Proceed to X-ray diffraction analysis.

## Next Steps

1. Harvest crystals using cryoprotection
2. Flash freeze in liquid nitrogen
3. Collect X-ray diffraction data
4. Determine protein structure
